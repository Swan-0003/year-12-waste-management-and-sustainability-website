Why it matters

01

Protecting the Environment

Litter and waste harm New Zealand's land and wildlife. A 2019 survey found that industrial areas had the highest litter levels, with over 250 items per 1,000 m². Managing waste properly helps keep our natural spaces clean and protects native biodiversity.

Photo by Tasso Mitsarakis on Unsplash

02

Reducing Greenhouse Gases

Waste in landfills creates methane, a powerful greenhouse gas. Since the 1990s, improvements like gas capture systems have cut landfill emissions in New Zealand by nearly 20%. Some landfills even turn that gas into energy, powering thousands of homes.

03

Building Sustainable Communities

In Auckland, groups and businesses are already showing what can be done. Rescued turns surplus food into new products, Citizen Collective brews beer from leftover bread, and Ripe Deli serves plant-based meals while cutting waste. Community groups like Local encourage people to share resources and live more sustainably, while brands such as Ceres Organics offer organic options nationwide. These efforts show how small changes add up to a big impact.
